Support

Coming to Refuge:

When you come to Refuge for the first time, you will be met with a support worker, who will listen and support you through admission to the Refuge. We know that it is not easy to have to leave your home and seek the support of the Refuge and we endeavour to make you as welcome and comfortable as possible while supporting you through this difficult time. Our aim is to help you as a woman, to work towards your own goals and we know that you may need time to reflect, and to think about your plans while remaining safe in Refuge.

We believe that all people, regardless of age, race, class, education, religion or sexual orientation have the right to a non-violent life. We also recognise that sometimes, that your sole need from our service maybe a period of respite and we respect your wishes in this. We offer six self-contained apartments which include your own kitchen/living room, bedroom and your own bathroom.

All our apartments are fully fitted with the necessary appliances needed for everyday living. If you do not have money on arrival, staff will support you in accessing financial support and we will ensure that neither you, or your children will go without while waiting for an income to be set up. If you do not have clothes, shoes or other basic necessities, we will also ensure that you will be looked after from our provisions.

The Refuge is very well supported by the community and we receive regular support by donations of clothes, toiletries, etc. When in Refuge, you will have a Support Worker available to sit, listen and support you. We, recognise that you have your own unique needs and wishes and we will try to support and facilitate your needs as best we can during your stay with us. We provide a range of information on finance, housing, children, court orders and social welfare.

ChildCare

In Refuge, we have a playroom which is available to you and your children during your stay. We appreciate that it is not only difficult for you but also your children to transition into life in Refuge.

We have a dedicated Child Care worker who will support both you and your children around this, often confusing and emotional time. We have a playroom with many different activities and toys to help the children to settle in and entertain them during the day.

Telephone Support

Available 9am to 12 midnight, Monday to Friday (including Bank Holidays)

1pm to 11pm, Saturday and Sunday

Our telephone support service will offer you a confidential space to access support and information around your situation. We also support family members, friends and professionals who have concerns about a person, who might be experience domestic abuse. We hope to empower women to better understand what may be happening for them and offer information and advocacy to gain a better understanding as to the options that may be available to them.

Our role is to believe and offer support around your situation, we understand the dynamics and impact of abuse and will not place blame on you in your situation. We appreciate how difficult it can be to pick up the phone and tell someone about what is happening. We will give you time and support during the phone call and respect your judgements on what you are comfortable in sharing.

Talking about your experiences with us, may give you the opportunity to share with someone the difficulties and hardships faced in an abusive relationship.

Court Accompaniment

The Courts can provide protection for victims of Domestic Violence. However, we understand that the Court System can be daunting for anyone, but we can help you through our Court Accompaniment Service.

This service (funded by the Department of Justice through the Commission for the Support of Victims of Crime) is free of charge and available to any woman who is affected by Domestic Violence and attending Family Law Court Hearings in the District, Circuit or High Courts sitting in County Kerry.

Our Outreach Support Workers are in attendance at the following Court venues in County Kerry:

Tralee, Listowel, Killarney, Killorglin, Dingle, Kenmare, Cahirciveen.


We can provide you with support through what can be a difficult process through:

- Information on the legal options that may be open to you

- A referral to the Legal Aid Board or Family Law Solicitor

- The completion of a Legal Aid Application Form

- Accompaniment to your appointment with your solicitor

- Accompaniment to the Court Clerk’s Office to complete paperwork for your Hearing

- Accompaniment to your Court Hearing(s)

- Follow-up support following your Court Hearing(s)

- Please feel free to contact us for more information on this service.

Outreach Support

Some women may not need or want to come to a refuge but still require information and support. If this is how you feel, you might like to talk to our Outreach Support Workers who are there to help you. An outreach support worker can drive to meet you so that you both can sit down and talk about your situation.

You may not feel ready to do anything but it can give you the opportunity to talk and have someone listen to you in a supportive and confidential way. The outreach support worker can work with you if you need information on welfare entitlements, alternative accommodation, legal advice, education and training etc.

This service is free of charge


This is available to women Monday to Friday in County Kerry. The Outreach service can be accessed by contacting the Refuge on:

066 712 9100

and arranging an appointment with an Outreach Worker.

Sometimes, if an Outreach Worker is not available, staff will arrange a call back. This will come up as a Private Number so no one can identify where the call is from.



Helping a friend or a family member

Helping a friend or a family member if they're experiencing domestic abuse.

If you're worried a friend is being abused, let them know you've noticed something is wrong. They might not be ready to talk but try to find quiet times when they can talk if they choose to. If someone confides in you that they're suffering domestic abuse:

  • listen, and take care not to blame them
  • acknowledge it takes strength to talk to someone about experiencing abuse
  • give them time to talk, but don't push them to talk if they don't want to
  • acknowledge they're in a frightening and difficult situation
  • tell them nobody deserves to be threatened or beaten, despite what the abuser has said
  • support them as a friend – encourage them to express their feelings, and allow them to make their own decisions
  • don't tell them to leave the relationship if they're not ready – that's their decision
  • ask if they have suffered physical harm – if so, offer to go with them to a hospital or GP
  • help them report the assault to the Gardai they choose to
  • Be ready to give them ADAPT’s contact details so they can access, free, confidential support
